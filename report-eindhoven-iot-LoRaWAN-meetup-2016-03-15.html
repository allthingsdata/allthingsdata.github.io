LoRaWAN IoT Meetup of 14 March 2016 of the Eindhoven IoT Meetup

Short, crude report of the Meetup by Peter Vandenabeele of All Things Data.

Hackaton Arduino, Rasberry Pie, LoRaWAN boards

From 17:00 to 19:00 people where having fun with boards and LoRaWAN set-ups.

Speaker 1: Dennis Tilgencamp (Storm Company) Starting up a biz in IoT

Dennis introduced Storm company with this slide:

Dennis explained the current status as a "lean" company, only contractors and partners.

He also explained how he sees IoT as a market and that the rapid rise of LoRaWAN was the trigger that pushed him to start it "this is it ...".

Criteria for LoRa are:

  • public (kpn + Belgacom) vs Private (own gateway on-site) vs Crowd (The Things Network etc.)
  • What is the required critical mass ? Will it reach it ? => LoRa gained a lot traction
  • Many other technologies: 2/3/4/5G, Narrowband IoT, Zigbee, BLE, ...

Speaker 3: Rene Schouwman from Inteliments

Rene showed this overview of the ecosystem in which he is working:

An example of an application he is selling is SOLARTHERM which measures the savings in gas that are realised with sun heating of the boiler

Another small demo application is indoor air quality (CO2, temp, humidity) and use that to drive the air conditioning (it was a very crude solution because the sensors where not very accurate in this version):

Technologies used in the different solutions are:

  • MQTT
  • LoraWAN
  • SigFox (very useful for static objects (e.g. crop field, humity)
  • IQRF MESH Network (for customers with very private data, not "in the cloud")

Security is becoming very important now ... Next to this, Inteliments also offers an IoT platform (IntelliGlue), Integrated connectivity to analytics and consulting.

An interesting note is that SigFOX stores the data in the cloud and you can ask it back via API. Also SigFOX has a "long" tranmission time. More applications discussed where Asset tracking (with GPS) of bulldozers, waste management of glass containers.
